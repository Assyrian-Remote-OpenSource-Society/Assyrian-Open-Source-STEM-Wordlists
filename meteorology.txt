Meteorology 
____

Weather, air, the fluid that we breathe, the atmosphere, oxygen - hawa - ܗܵܘܵܐ
– very common; shared with: Arabic, Turkish, Persian, Kurdish, Hindi

Weather, air - athir - ܐܵܬ݂ܝܼܪ

Wind - pokha - ܦܵܘܚܵܐ

Windy, breezy - pokhanta/pokhana - ܦܵܘܚܵܢܵܐ

Water - mia - ܡ̈ܝܼܵܐ

Rain  - midraa - ܡܸܛܪܵܐ

Rainy - midraanaa - ܡܸܛܪܵܢܵܐ

Wet, rainy, watered with rain - m'tira - ܡܛܝܼܪܵܐ

Rainy weather, showery, wet - ar'ianta / ar'iana - ܥܵܪܝܵܢܬܵܐ / ܥܵܪܝܵܢܵܐ

A storm - ki'moona - ܟܹܐܡܘܼܢܵܐ

Lightning - birqa - ܒܸܪܩܵܐ
Lightning - zalqa - ܙܲܠܩܵܐ

Snow - talga - ܬܲܠܓܵܐ

To snow - ari'talga - ܥܵܪܹܐ ܬܲܠܓܵܐ

Sleet - zu'gana - ܙܘܼܩܵܢܵܐ
Sleet - qu'zaia - ܩܘܼܙܵܝܵܐ

Flood, an overflow - ga'khoota - ܓܵܚܘܼܬܵܐ

Tornado - zoe'za-a - ܙܘܿܥܙܵܥܵܐ

Earthquake, a seism, a vibration - no’dana - ܢܵܘܕܵܢܵܐ

Avalanche, a snowslide - a'shitha - ܥܵܫܝܼܬ݂ܵܐ

Volcano - gasi'noora - ܓܵܣܹܐ ܢܘܼܪܵܐ

Rainbow - qish'taai'maraan - ܩܸܫܬܲܝ ܡܵܪܲܢ

Sky, heaven, the firmament - shmaya - ܫܡܲܝܵܐ

Skies - shma'iatha - ܫܡ̈ܲܝܵܬ݂ܵܐ

A clear sky, fine weather - saakhwa - ܨܲܚܘܵܐ

To clear up, to become transparent - spa - ܨܦܵܐ

Cloud - aaywaa/aayvaa - ܫܡ̈ܲܝܵܬ݂ܵܐ

Cloudy - aay'vaanaa - ܥܲܝܒ݂ܵܢܵܐ

Dusky - ba'hoora - ܒܲܗܘܼܪܵܐ

Gloomy, dusky, dismal through obscurity or darkness - makm'hana - ܡܲܟܡܗܵܢܵܐ

Sun - shimsha - ܫܸܡܫܵܐ

Sunny - shimsha’na - ܫܸܡܫܵܢܵܐ

Sunset - ginet shimsha -  ܓܢܵܝܬ ܫܸܡܫܵܐ

Nightfall, close of the day - ginet yooma - ܓܢܵܝܬ ܝܵܘܡܵܐ

The moon - sahra -ܣܲܗܪܵܐ

Full moon (filled moon) - kis sah’malia - ܟܸܣܐܵܐ ܡܲܠܝܵܐ

Moonlight - shimshi’sara - ܫܸܡܫܝܼ ܣܲܗܪܵܐ

The Earth, the world, the universe - doonyi - ܕܘܼܢܝܹܐ
[Variants of the above word is shared between Arabic, Farsi, Kurdish, Turkish, and Hindi.]

Earthly, of the world - doo'nyata - ܕܘܼܢܝܵܝܬܵܐ

Outer space - atir ba'raia - ܐܵܬܝܼܪ ܒܲܪܵܝܵܐ

Meteor, a transient luminous body in the sky, a shooting star, a falling star, a fireball - daq'disa - ܕܲܐܩܕܝܼܣܵܐ

Summer - qeta - ܩܲܝܛܵܐ

Spring - be’nisane - ܒܢܝܼܣܵܢ

Winter - sitwa - ܣܸܬܘܵܐ

Autumn - chiri - ܟ̰ܸܪܝܼܹܐ
Autumn - tish'riyi - ܬܸܫܪ̈ܝܹܐ
